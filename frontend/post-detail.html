The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris. Named after its designer, Gustave Eiffel, it was constructed from 1887 to 1889 and is one of the most recognizable structures in the world. At 330 meters tall, it was the world's tallest man-made structure for 41 years.
